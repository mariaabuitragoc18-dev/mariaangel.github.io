Vanadium

vanadium is a metallic chemical element with atomic number23 . it is a tough, ductile, silvery transition metal that occurs in several minerals and is widely used in alloys to strengthen steel

main properties

Vanadium is a solid element at room temperature and is found mainly in minerals such as patronite and vanadinite. It is of great importance in the metallurgical industry due to its use in alloys.

  • Symbol: V
  • Númber atómic: 23
  • atómic mass: 50.9415 u
  • state: metállic solid
  • Color: silvery gray

Laboratory Analysis Summary

The experiment focused on determining the concentration of vanadium in different samples using spectrophotometry. Changes in color were observed to identify the oxidation states of vanadium: V(II), V(III), V(IV), and V(V). The most common species found were V(IV) (blue-green color) and V(V) (yellow color).

Experimental Data

Sample Absorbance (420 nm) Calculated Concentration (mg/L) Observation
Sample A 0.254 1.59 Blue-green color, indicating V(IV)
Sample B 0.610 3.80 Yellow color, indicating V(V)
Sample C 0.130 0.78 Light blue color, possible V(III)

The obtained concentrations ranged between 0.78 and 3.80 mg/L, showing a consistent calibration curve and reliable absorbance readings. The experiment confirmed that vanadium can exist in multiple oxidation states, easily identifiable by color changes.
